Research

  1. [Ongoing] Constraining vs. Regularizing Objectives for Multi-Objective Machine Learning. Collaborating with Jose Gallego-Posada, Manuel Del Verme and Nikolaus Howe from Mila.

    Teaser

    We base our project on the observation that machine learning practitioners tend to optimize a linear combination of several objectives without strong theoretical justification for doing so, or without following a principled method to select the multiplicative factors used to combine these terms. We make a case for solving a constrained formulation of the problem, and are empirically testing whether our approach is beneficial on a wide range of machine learning applications.

  2. Data Augmentation via Geodesic Interpolation on the Latent Space of Deep Generative Models. Under the Supervision of Jose Gallego-Posada from Mila.

    Explored deep generative models and the interpolation of latent representations as a means for data augmentation. Instead of doing interpolations via linear combinations of samples, we favoured taking into account the curvature of the latent space, given the Riemannian metric induced by the Jacobian of the generator network. I handled most of the coding and experimentation for this project, which served as my first exposure to research.
